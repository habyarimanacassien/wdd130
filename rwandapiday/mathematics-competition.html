2023 Rwanda Mathematics Competition

The Pi Day Mathematics Competition is an opportunity for secondary/ high school students in Rwanda's schools majoring in Mathematics to explore the fun side of mathematics. The students compete in three stages from district level to national level. The district level competition will determine the top students from the participated ones who will go to provincial level. The top students from the proncial competition advance to the final round, which is the national competition.

Rules and Regulations

At each stage, the test consists of 30 multiple-choice questions. For each of the questions mark with cross (x) the alternative answer which you find is true. If you, at some point, want to change the marking add a clear note. Only one of the alternatives A, B, C, D and E is true. No calculators, cellphones nor other electronic devices are allowed in the test. Only paper, pen, eraser, straightedge and compass.

Please do not make any calculation on the problem pages. Those are only for answers. For your own calculations use scratch papers. Students' movements in the test room are not allowed after the exam started. Students communicating with each other during the test will be removed from the room. The total time allowed for the test is 3 hours.

Competition venue

The venue for both competition levels will be communicated at least 7 days before the competition took place.

Prizes

Certificates of Participation will be given to all participants in the final stage. More prestigious prizes will be awarded to finalists (top 5 girls and 5 boys) at an award ceremony to be held immediately after the completion of the Final round on 14 March.
